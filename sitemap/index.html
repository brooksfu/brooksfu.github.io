    Single-neuron mechanisms of error self-monitoring

    To err is human. In our daily life, we often make errors that deviates from our goals, caused by interference or distraction. These “oops” or “oh shoot” moments are all too familiar to us. Our brains, remarkably, can monitor these self-made errors and generate an error signal. What are the mechanisms?



    The geometry of domain-general performance monitoring

    Humans excel at performing a wide array of difficult cognitive tasks. During these difficult tasks, we often need to overcome distraction, monitor our own errors when we failed to overcome distraction, knows how fast or slowly we have performed. Our performance monitoring system is thus “domain general”. At the same time, to compensate for these disturbances to our goals, we need specific measures. How can the brain achieve both, i.e., generalizability and specialization, at the same time?
